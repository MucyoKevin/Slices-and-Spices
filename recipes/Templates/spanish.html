Paella

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp Olive oil
  • 1 Onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 Red bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 Tomatoes, chopped
  • 200 g Chorizo, sliced
  • 300 g Arborio rice or short-grain paella rice
  • 1 tsp Smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp Saffron threads, soaked in 2 tbsp warm water
  • 1 liter Chicken or seafood stock
  • 200 g Chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 200 g Prawns, peeled and deveined
  • 200 g Mussels, cleaned
  • 100 g Green peas
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large paella pan or a wide, deep skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and cook until it becomes translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in the garlic and red bell pepper, and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and sliced chorizo, cooking until the tomatoes have softened and the chorizo is slightly browned.
  5. Stir in the rice, making sure it is well coated with the oil and vegetables.
  6. Add the smoked paprika and the saffron with its soaking water, stirring to combine.
  7. Pour in the chicken or seafood stock, stirring well to distribute the ingredients evenly.
  8. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 10 minutes, uncovered.
  9. Add the chicken pieces, prawns, mussels, and green peas, nestling them into the rice mixture. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Continue to cook the paella, without stirring, for about 15-20 minutes, until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed. The bottom should form a nice, crispy layer called "socarrat."
  11. If the rice is not fully cooked, add a little more stock or water and cook for a few more minutes.
  12. Once cooked, remove the pan from the heat, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let the paella rest for about 5 minutes.
  13. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges on the side.
