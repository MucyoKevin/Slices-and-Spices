Ethiopian Cuisine

Discover the rich flavors and vibrant spices of Ethiopian cuisine...

Injera

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Teff flour
  • 3 cups Water
  • 1/4 tsp Salt
  • Optional: Yeast (if fermenting for a shorter time)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix the teff flour with 2 cups of water to create a batter. Cover and let it sit at room temperature for 2 to 3 days to ferment.
  2. Once the batter is bubbly and has a slightly sour smell, add the remaining water and salt, then mix well.
  3. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Pour a thin layer of batter, covering the entire surface of the skillet.
  4. Cover the skillet with a lid and cook for about 1-2 minutes until the injera is cooked through and small bubbles form on the surface.
  5. Remove the injera from the skillet and let it cool. Repeat with the remaining batter.
  6. Injera is traditionally served with various stews and salads on top.

Doro Wat

Ingredients

  • 1.5 kg Chicken, cut into pieces
  • 3 large Onions, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup Niter Kibbeh (spiced clarified butter)
  • 3 tbsp Berbere spice mix
  • 2 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp Ginger, minced
  • 1 tsp Ground cardamom
  • 1 tsp Ground fenugreek
  • 1 tsp Paprika
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 4 Hard-boiled eggs, peeled
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Water as needed

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken pieces with lemon juice and a pinch of salt. Set aside.
  2. In a large pot, cook the onions over medium heat without oil, stirring frequently until they are softened and golden brown.
  3. Add the niter kibbeh and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Stir in the garlic, ginger, and berbere spice mix. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring constantly to avoid burning the spices.
  5. Add the marinated chicken to the pot, ensuring each piece is well-coated with the spice mixture.
  6. Pour in enough water to cover the chicken. Add the cardamom, fenugreek, paprika, and salt. Bring to a simmer.
  7. Cover the pot and cook over low heat for about 45 minutes to an hour, until the chicken is tender.
  8. Add the hard-boiled eggs during the last 15 minutes of cooking, allowing them to absorb the flavors.
  9. Adjust seasoning if necessary. Serve the Doro Wat hot with Injera.
